KPI Governance Page

Governed KPI layer for AI-assisted CS weekly business review. Answers: can we trust these KPIs, how are they defined, and can AI safely use them?

Semantic layer: models/semantic/semantic_cs_kpi_metrics.yml Quality status: 11/11 PASS Synthetic/mock portfolio project. No real customer, employee, financial, employer, or proprietary company data is used.

Executive summary

One governed semantic KPI layer supports automated HTML reporting, weekly diagnostics, KPI governance documentation, and future BI implementation.

AI-safe usage policy

AI may use the aggregated mart, metric definitions, data quality status, and analyst validation notes. AI should not access raw customer, agent, free-text, employer, or proprietary data.

Contact volume
contact_volume		 Number of contacts created in the week.
count(contacts)		 week, country, contact reason CS Operations Analytics Weekly Context-dependent Volume increases can reflect demand growth or upstream friction.
Contact rate
contact_rate		 Contacts divided by total orders.
contact_volume / total_orders		 week, country, contact reason with country-week order denominator CS Operations Analytics Weekly Lower Order volume repeats across reason-level rows; interpret at country-week or with reason mix context.
AHT
avg_aht_minutes		 Average handling time in minutes for resolved contacts.
sum(handle_minutes) / resolved_contacts		 week, country, contact reason CS Operations Analytics Weekly Lower Sensitive to contact complexity, agent mix, and escalation mix.
FCR
fcr_rate		 Resolved contacts not reopened within the observation window divided by resolved contacts.
first_contact_resolved_contacts / resolved_contacts		 week, country, contact reason CS Operations Analytics Weekly Higher Can move with policy complexity and downstream issue recurrence.

avg_csat		 Average submitted CSAT score linked to contacts.

backlog_end_of_week		 Contacts created in the week that remain open at week end.
count(open_contacts_at_week_end)		 week, country, contact reason CS Operations Analytics Weekly Lower Backlog reflects both demand pressure and staffing capacity.
